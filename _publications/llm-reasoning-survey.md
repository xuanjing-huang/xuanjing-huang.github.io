Reasoning is a fundamental capability that distinguishes human intelligence from simple pattern matching. With the rapid development of large language models (LLMs), there has been increasing interest in their reasoning capabilities and potential applications. This survey provides a comprehensive overview of the current state of reasoning in LLMs, covering recent progresses, frontiers, and challenges. We systematically categorize and analyze different types of reasoning tasks, including mathematical reasoning, logical reasoning, commonsense reasoning, and causal reasoning. We examine various approaches to enhance reasoning capabilities, such as chain-of-thought prompting, self-consistency, and tool-augmented reasoning. Furthermore, we discuss the theoretical foundations of reasoning in LLMs and explore the relationship between model scale, training data, and reasoning performance. We also identify key challenges and future directions, including the need for better evaluation metrics, more robust reasoning mechanisms, and deeper understanding of the underlying principles. This survey aims to provide researchers with a comprehensive understanding of the current landscape and future opportunities in LLM reasoning research.
