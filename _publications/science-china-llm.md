Large language models (LLMs) have emerged as a transformative technology in artificial intelligence, demonstrating remarkable capabilities across a wide range of natural language processing tasks. This paper provides a comprehensive overview of the current state of large language models, covering fundamental techniques, diverse applications, and emerging challenges. We begin by introducing the architectural foundations and training methodologies that enable the development of powerful LLMs. We then explore various techniques for improving model performance, including pre-training strategies, fine-tuning approaches, and alignment methods. The paper surveys a broad spectrum of applications where LLMs have shown significant impact, from traditional NLP tasks to emerging domains such as code generation, scientific research, and creative writing. We also discuss the challenges associated with LLMs, including computational requirements, ethical considerations, safety concerns, and evaluation methodologies. Furthermore, we examine recent advances in efficient training and inference techniques, as well as approaches to enhance model interpretability and controllability. This comprehensive survey aims to provide researchers and practitioners with a thorough understanding of the current landscape and future directions in large language model research and development.
